The farm-equipment economy is bottoming — and public data sees it before revenue does

The big idea

The farm-equipment supply chain that feeds the marketplaces has contracted about 22% since 2022 and is only now stabilizing. These public, upstream series are correlated with marketplace activity and have historically moved ahead of it — offering an early read on listing, auction, and ad-spend pressure before it reaches the income statement.

How the market is connected — and where each data source fits

Purpose. Test whether public, upstream economic data can serve as an early-warning layer for marketplace volume — and quantify how deep today's equipment downturn runs and whether it is still worsening. Four U.S. monthly series were pulled, cleaned, modeled, and analyzed in BigQuery SQL.

Recommendations

The public signal is built and validated. The next three steps turn it into an operational early-warning capability — and each one is sharper with internal data.

1

Validate the signal against internal data

Compare listing counts, auction sell-through, and dealer ad spend against these upstream indicators to measure how their timing lines up, then stand it up as a live early-warning monitor. This converts a public proxy into a quantified read on revenue conditions ahead of the income statement.

2

Extend the model across every vertical

Replicate this exact pipeline for construction, trucking, and aviation equipment. If all verticals are correlated with the same macro factors, that quantifies concentration risk — the possibility that one rate cycle is associated with pressure across every revenue line at once.

3

Set threshold triggers for action

Define months-of-inventory and year-over-year deceleration levels that prompt budget or sales-effort reallocation. This replaces reacting to a soft quarter with acting on a leading indicator ahead of it.

Data & method

Four public monthly series, retrieved as CSV from FRED, modeled in BigQuery as a star schema with window-function metrics. Every input was verified clean — no nulls, no duplicate dates, no month gaps — before modeling.

Pipeline: raw inspection → cleaning (SAFE_CAST, status flag, deduplication guard) → UNION ALL into a tidy long fact → star schema (date and series dimensions) → window-function metrics (LAG for month-over-month and year-over-year, an expanding MAX for drawdown, FIRST_VALUE for rebasing) → long and wide analysis views feeding the visuals above. The lag analysis correlates detrended (year-over-year) series at 0–12-month offsets.

Scope. These are manufacturer and macro series — an upstream proxy for marketplace activity, not internal listing or auction data — and the analysis covers the agricultural vertical. All relationships reported are correlations and timing offsets, not causal estimates. That scope defines the recommendations above.
